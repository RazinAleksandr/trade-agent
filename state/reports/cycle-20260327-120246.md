## Learnings

1. Sports matchup and spread markets consistently show edges below 2% across three consecutive cycles, confirming they are efficiently priced by sportsbooks and should be excluded from the analysis pipeline to conserve analyst compute.
2. Long-dated political markets (end date >1 year) like the Newsom 2028 nomination market passed the sweet-spot price filter but were correctly rejected -- the pre-analysis filtering should incorporate end-date checks to avoid wasting analyst time.
3. All 6 analyzed markets this cycle had edges below 0.055, suggesting the current market environment (high-volume, well-followed markets) may offer fewer exploitable mispricings than the system's first cycle which found 14-20% edges on geopolitical events.
4. The Risk Manager correctly identified correlation between the March 31 Iran ground forces market and the existing April 30 Iran position -- this cross-position awareness is working well and should be maintained.
5. Web search provided strong signal for the geopolitical analyst outputs (Iran ceasefire, Iran ground forces) with well-cited evidence from credible sources, but for sports markets the analyst essentially replicated sportsbook pricing, adding no incremental value.

## Strategy Suggestions

1. **Exclude single-game sports markets from analysis pipeline.** Add explicit category-based pre-filtering in the scanner or sweet-spot step: exclude single-game sports matchup markets and spread markets, which have never cleared the 0.10 edge threshold across three cycles. This will free analyst capacity for higher-edge-potential market categories.
2. **Add end-date pre-filtering.** Exclude markets with end dates more than 90 days from now during the sweet-spot step unless they show extreme volume anomalies or obvious mispricing (YES price between 0.30-0.70 despite being very likely or unlikely events).
3. **Widen the scanning funnel.** Consider scanning a wider set of markets (increase --limit from 20 to 40) and applying more aggressive pre-filtering to find the rarer markets where Polymarket pricing diverges significantly from informed estimates. The 0.10 edge threshold is appropriate, but the funnel needs to be wider at the top to find qualifying opportunities.
