## Learnings

1. Esports H2H records (even with 7+ match samples) proved unreliable as a primary edge signal -- Aurora won 2-0 despite TheMongolz's 7-2 historical advantage, resulting in a total loss. The strategy rule promoting esports analysis should be downgraded or removed.

2. The 0.10 minimum edge threshold correctly prevented four marginal trades this cycle. The largest edge found was 4.5% on the Iran ceasefire market, which would have added correlated Iran exposure to an already Iran-heavy portfolio.

3. Geopolitical markets with strong evidential basis (concrete events, authoritative reporting) produce excellent Brier scores (0.04 on Israel-Lebanon) while speculative markets relying on statistical proxies produce poor ones (0.384 on CS:GO).

4. The current market environment (dominated by Iran conflict and sports events) offers limited edge opportunities -- 21 markets scanned, 4 analyzed, none cleared the threshold. This is the second consecutive zero-trade cycle.

5. Position monitoring and timely sells are generating real value: the Israel-Lebanon sell captured $62.49 in realized profit by exiting at 0.999 before formal resolution. The position monitor step is a critical part of the P&L pipeline and should remain a first-class step in every cycle.

## Strategy Suggestions

1. **Downgrade esports strategy rule.** Replace strategy rule 4 ("Esports and niche markets deserve analysis time") with a more cautious version: "Esports markets may be analyzed but should require higher edge thresholds (0.15+) and should not rely primarily on historical H2H records as edge justification." The CS:GO loss with Brier 0.384 demonstrates that H2H data is not a reliable edge source in esports.

2. **Implement time-to-resolution adjusted edge threshold.** For markets resolving within 7 days with analyst confidence above 0.70, reduce MIN_EDGE_THRESHOLD to 0.07. This cycle's Iran invasion (March 31) market had 0.72 confidence and 4% edge with only 4 days to resolution. Near-term events have more precise probability estimates, and the time-decay risk of holding the position is lower, justifying a thinner edge requirement.

3. **Add dead-position cleanup logic.** The CS:GO position (market price 0.0005, no liquidity) should be automatically marked as a realized loss when price drops below 0.01 with no bid liquidity. Carrying it as an open position distorts portfolio utilization metrics (currently showing 3 positions at 29.3% utilization when the real picture is 2 positions at 25.4%).
